# DispatchDesk Logistics Standard Operating Procedures (Logistics)

## SOP-DEL-01: Shipment Delay — Customer Notification
When a shipment's ETA slips by more than 30 minutes:
1. Dispatch updates the shipment status to "Delayed" in the TMS with the delay cause code (weather, traffic, mechanical, carrier, customs, other).
2. Notify the customer contact by phone AND email within 60 minutes of identifying the delay. Include the revised ETA and delay cause.
3. Log the notification time in the shipment notes.
4. For URGENT-priority shipments, notify the customer within 15 minutes and copy the account manager.

## SOP-DEL-02: Delay Escalation
If a delay exceeds 4 hours, or any URGENT shipment is delayed:
1. Escalate to the dispatch supervisor immediately.
2. The supervisor evaluates recovery options: reroute, load transfer to backup truck, or partner carrier substitution.
3. Document the recovery decision in the shipment notes within 30 minutes.
4. Send the customer a recovery plan, not just a new ETA.

## SOP-DMG-01: Damaged Shipment Report
To file a damaged shipment report:
1. Stop unloading and photograph the damage before moving the freight (minimum 4 photos: wide shot, close-up, label/ID visible, packaging condition).
2. Note the damage on the proof of delivery (POD) before the receiver signs. Never accept a clean signature on damaged freight.
3. File form DR (Damage Report) in the TMS within 2 hours of discovery, attaching photos.
4. Notify the customer and the claims coordinator (claims@dispatchdesk.example) the same business day.
5. Retain damaged packaging until the claim is closed.

## SOP-DOC-04: Missing Customs / Import Documentation
If a shipment is missing customs or import paperwork (e.g., FDA import release, commercial invoice):
1. Place the shipment in "Exception" status — do NOT dispatch.
2. Contact the compliance team (compliance@dispatchdesk.example) with the shipment ID and missing document list.
3. Notify the customer within 4 business hours with what is needed and from whom.
4. Resume transit only after compliance clears the hold in writing.

## SOP-COLD-01: Temperature-Controlled (Cold Chain) Deliveries
1. Verify reefer unit temperature at pickup and record it in the TMS (pharmacy loads: 2–8°C).
2. Re-check temperature at any stop longer than 30 minutes.
3. At delivery, scan items at the customer's designated intake point (for hospitals, the pharmacy intake — not the main dock).
4. If temperature goes out of range at any point, mark the shipment "Exception" and call the customer before delivering. Out-of-range pharmacy loads must not be delivered without written customer approval.

## SOP-SEC-01: Secure-Facility Deliveries
For deliveries to secure or controlled facilities (defense, government):
1. Confirm the delivery appointment and dock number 24 hours in advance.
2. Drivers must carry a valid TWIC card or facility-issued credential.
3. No photos may be taken on-site. Phones remain in the cab.
4. Any gate refusal is reported to dispatch immediately; do not wait at the gate longer than 30 minutes without instruction.
